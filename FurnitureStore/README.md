# FURNITURE STORE MINI PROJECT
## About Project
Despite recording 10,000 transactions, the furniture store faces a strategic blind spot regarding its regional market reach. To map out core revenue drivers and uncover which regions urgently need targeted marketing, this analysis is divided into three stages:
* Data Cleaning
* Aggregating & Plotting
* Data Visualization

## Project Dataset
Data Toko Furniture.csv by Karirnex (Data 2025)

## Analysis Results
* A positive correlation exists between unit price and total revenue, indicating that higher unit prices generally yield higher revenue. This trend is evident in the scatter plot, where the data points rise from the bottom left toward the top right.
* A flat discount rate of 10% is applied uniformly across all product categories, showing zero variation in the discount distribution. This suggests that the store does not employ targeted promotional strategies or category based discount differentiation.

## Business Insights
* The Jakarta region serves as the revenue backbone, which is likely due to the high purchasing power of the community and its position as an economic center (North Jakarta, West Jakarta, and Central Jakarta).
* Satellite cities, such as Depok and Bogor, outperform other Jakarta areas (South Jakarta and East Jakarta), demonstrating that the target market outside the capital also has strong demand.
* The South Tangerang, Tangerang, and Bekasi regions generate massive revenue despite ranking at the bottom, but evaluation is still needed as they have the potential for growth.

## Actionable Recommendations
* Focus on local marketing by intensively conducting promotions or adding distribution points in satellite regions, such as South Tangerang, Tangerang, and Bekasi, to close the revenue gap.
* Allocate more resources to the markets in Depok and Bogor, as their potential has been proven.
